As of now, this page will be focusing on the first game in the series, translated as "Danganronpa: Trigger Happy Havoc"

ダンガンロンパ

'Danganronpa' is a series of Japanese video games developed by Spike Chunsoft, that deal with a group of high school students who are forced into a killing-game, where in order to escape, they have to kill one of their classmates and get away with it. The games are presented in a decidedly trope-y anime style, but frequently deal with complex and surprisingly dark and serious themes. All of the students you meet throughout the three main games are considered "Ultimates," aka students who are the best in the world at something. Examples include the ultimate baseball star, the ultimate pop idol, the ultimate lucky student, and the ultimate clairvoyant. While some talents are played for laughs, most are used creatively to enhance each character's story, and are even sometimes incorporated into the convoluted murders you have to solve in each chapter.

There are three main games in the series, with a several spinoffs (including an anime), as well as different game modes once the player has beaten the main story at least once. The main stories of each game are presented in a visual novel format that transitions to action/logic gameplay for the class trial sequences, which are largely the most well-known aspects of the series, and serve as the finales for each chapter. Each chapter has you solving the murder of at least one of your classmates, as well as trying to solve the larger mystery of who, or what, is controlling this killing game.

Joel Liesenberg (he/him/his), University of Michigan School of Information/Center for Japanese Studies, 2022
