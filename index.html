Welcome

Hello all, and welcome to my translation journal. My name is Joel Liesenberg, and I am currently a dual-degree Master's student at the University of Michigan, studying Japanese studies and Information Science, focusing in Japanese digital humanities. As an undergraduate student at Central Michigan University, I began to fall in love with both Japanese and linguistics, and after living in Japan for two years as part of the JET Program after graduation, I developed a fascination with the art of translation. Since then, I have tried to practice translation in my free time as part of my language study, and this website is a way for me to share my work.

This website houses several pages, each of which offer a look into some current translation projects I have undertaken recently. On every page is a brief explanation of the material, its medium, its intended audience, and other pertinent information. After that is the journal content, which is meant to be a thought-process walkthrough as I work with the materials, along with examples of passages/vocabulary/grammar points that gave me a particular challenge. These are by no means official translations in any capability. This website is just meant to be a repository to collect my scraps of thought, and provide a framework to organize my various personal projects. There is also a gallery page filled with extra images of each project and pictures of my notes.

Joel Liesenberg (he/him/his), University of Michigan School of Information/Center for Japanese Studies, 2022
